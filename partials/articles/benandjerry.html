I admit it. Having kids terrified me. I’m what you call a womb-worrier. I started worrying about the little devils from the second I knew I was with child.

The Redneck and Hannah

If you have kids I’ll bet your wish list is similar to mine.

  • Let them have a safe place to live.
  • Let them be healthy and happy.
  • Let them learn well in school.
  • Let them not meet a guy from a band, dye their hair into a purple mohawk and run away to California to live in a van.

Along with kids came the stress that I will lose my job and not being able to take care of them.

Every day I read about some company somewhere having to cut costs and let people go. Since I got married and had children I’ve been laid off twice and gone to jail once. Ok. I made up that last part. I thought it might make me sound spicy.

We’ve had one advantage though…I’ve unfortunately worked for companies where I knew I might get laid off the week after I started. It kept us in a constant state of preparedness that I don’t think will ever wear off.

When I was pregnant with my second child I had this conversation with my very considerate boss.

Boss: (Intense look on her face.) Amy. When are you due?
Me: In about a month.
Boss: Ok. You need to go to your Dr. and tell her you need to get induced two weeks early.
Me: (Intense look on my face.) Ok. Why?
Boss: Because we’ll be laid off by your due date and you won’t have your insurance.

I sat in my bed that night eating half-off Ben & Jerry’s ice cream…stewing.

As much as I tried to not panic I could not stop myself.

I was wondering… what in the hell are we going to do?

How will we pay the mortgage?

Do I need a special “I just got laid off” identity card to get into the food bank?

Do they have Ben & Jerry’s?

In the end it worked out ok. We did not have mountains of money in our savings but we had enough to last 3 months with some left over.

The new job I took paid a lot less so I took a job writing at night to supplement my income. But…everything was OK.

We weren’t rich but we’d saved enough to get through the crisis.

When I ask myself why I am “cheap” that time-frame is what I remember.

Being cheap is a kind of insurance for me. Can I keep my family safe and fed even if the crap hits the fan? Will I have options or will I have to get a managerial position at Burger King and bring home left-over whoppers for dinner?

I still worry about all the things I worried about before…but I prepare. That way…I don’t HAVE to worry about the money part as much.

I know things happen I can’t prepare for. Saving money is something I can control if I make it a priority.

I can play with my kids and not have that nagging terror in the back of my mind. I can spend my energy on other things…like studying with them or watching them do kung-foo with my Redneck Husband. And…I don’t have to worry if the food bank has Cherry Garcia.
